Employer's Playground

A soft, cinematic feature in seven chapters · May 2026

I watch her work. I see the way her shoulders hunch over the mop, the way she scrubs the same spot for too long, like she’s trying to erase something invisible. I see her checking her phone, terrified of making a sound, waiting for the silence so she can disappear into her room. That hesitation—that fear—is delicious. It’s my currency.

I know she’s tired. I know she’s lonely. I know she thinks she’s a burden to everyone around her. That is exactly where I want her. That is where she is most useful.

A quiet frame at the edge of the day.

It’s late. The house is quiet. I call her into my office, not to discuss work, but to "help her relax." I tell her she’s been working too hard and that I want to spoil her a little. She’s used to doing things for me, but this is different. This is the lever I pull. I use her guilt. "You’re so stressed, Joyce. But you’re so good at taking care of everyone else. Let me take care of you tonight. You don’t have to think about anything."
